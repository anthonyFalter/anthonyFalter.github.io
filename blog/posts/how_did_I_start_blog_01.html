How did I get started? Talent or Hard Work?

July 24, 2026, 10:55 AM · Ross Elopre



At one point in our life, we all face the question: "Is what I am doing enough?", "Why are other people better than me?" or, "Will I succeed?". Trust me, these questions have plagued me for a very long time.

We all have that one moment at school where a teacher gives a very challenging assignment, and we feel overwhelmed. But for some reason? that one guy finishes it with seamingly no effort wasted. What will your perception be? Admiration? Curiosity?

Well for me, It is a combination of both. I believe that a talented person never took things for granted, and took the path of hardship to attain their goals. Just like how that one guy finished that challenging assignment, He spent a lot of time understanding how each part of the assignment works and did the heavy-lifting of thinking solutions to the problem.

At one point in my life, I've been told I was "Talented". That I was a "Genius" when it comes to tech stuffs (Yes I am a big nerd to tech stuffs). There are times when my professors gave our class a very hard problem to solve using Java and fortunately, I was able to answer it. But was it Talent?

Spoiler alert: Nope. It was hard work.



Ever heard of Schrödinger's cat? It is basically a thought experiment on how an experimental cat inside a radioactive chamber can both be alive and dead at the same time.

Well, I gave that as an example since it illustrates the concept of uncertainty and the impact of observation on reality. Just like how my peers observed my knack in programming as a mystery, They haven't observed the sheer hardwork I have put in towards the skills that was able to contribute to that scenario.

I was either that common trope in anime in which a lazy guy is actually a genius who puts zero effort but still achieves exceptionally high grades in exam. Or, I was that one guy with no ambitions and no goals to achieve for.

The best part? I was neither. I am just a person who strives to learn every single thing. A person with the greed for curiosity. And, a person with the determination to pursue my goals and achieve success.



So, how did I really start in the data science field?

Well, it all started during my elementary days (I was 9), back when I was reading a popular magazine for the youngsters called "K-Zone" which, is now discontinued Rip KZone, you will always be remembered :(.

There was this single section in that magazine that covered Construct 2, a game development engine for 2D games. My brain itched for the curiosity to learn how variables work and how actions trigger a function.

I was reading it at school when I saw that section, and from the moment I saw it, I was hooked and was itching to go home right away to explore it in our family computer.

So, fast forward to my dismissal when I was already at my home, I went and searched it on the internet. And fortunately, It had a trial version enough to satisfy my curiosity.

I spent countless hours learning the fundamentals from scratch without documentation because of my innate stubbornness. And instead, learning the functions by trial and errors.

So, curious of what was the very first project I made in Construct 2? Well, it was pong. A simple 2 player game where you control a moving platform that bounces a ball.

I just did the programming side of the game using only code blocks, since I was terrified of math and coding during that time (feel me?).

This was the very first moment my logical thinking was put to use and it really did spark my interest in programming from this point onwards.

I really enjoyed how decisions shaped the outcome of the game, and how a simple if/else statement could change everything in the game's environment.

The second big project I made was the ambitious Ragdoll game project in which objects can be combined to create more complex entities, (e.g: combining gas tanks to stove to create gas-fueled stove, etc) which, was actually recorded in my mom's laptop and is available on Youtube! Check here please don't mention my voice, I get cringed out.



Fast forward to 5 years later when I was in high school, I was introduced to Lua-C, the language of Roblox game development.

During this time, I was still terrified of coding, and math stuffs. But due to my curiosity, I decided to give it a shot. I studied how literal functions work, what each data type serves and how for loops can iterate n amount of times to help ease the manual iteration of each steps.

Well, to be honest with you, I felt like I didn't really have the natural aptitude for it. I REALLY felt so bad, knowing that I was uncertain whether I would reach my dream of becoming a programmer, or fail trying.

In Lua-C, especially in Trajectory calculations for projectiles, It used a combination of trigonometric functions and vector mathematics to calculate the path of the projectile. And during that time, just seeing a peek of the math formulas needed for calculations made me realize how bad I am at math. (Well, atleast I enjoyed the process of logical reasoning when it comes to designing data structures)

Thanks to my resilience, I was able to overcome the hurdles I faced with math in the programming field, and decided to face it head-on. By next year in that time, I was already implementing more mathematical operations to my workflow in projectiles of the gun script I was making.

Day by day, I learned new concepts and improved my skills in programming. It was at this time as well when I was exposed to Data, especially the Datastore feature of Roblox development where you manage key-value pairs of data to save each player's progress to the cloud. This sparked my interest in the data science field, after I saw a glimpse of how data can be used for insights and data-driven decision making.



And then, College came.

During my freshmen year, I was anxious of whether I would be able to keep up with my peers and the rigorous courseworks while still maintaining my passion for programming. To my surprise during our first lecture, all the things that are getting mentioned were stuffs I have already learned years ago when I was doing side-quests in programming!, It really felt like a miracle to me knowing that I would be able to keep up with my peers, while also maintaining my passion for creating things during my free time.

Thanks to my past experience and the sheer resilience for hardwork, I was able to excel in my studies, specifically in the areas of data structures and algorithms, and programming subjects in general. I am still bad at math, but I am now determined to get better for it.

There came a time when I realized that my passion for programming was not just a mere hobby, but now a calling. We were given an option to choose from 3 major options to major on our Junior year: Application Development, Business Intelligence, or the Infrastructures.

Due to my past works, It was only natural that I'd choose Application Development since I have built my foundation in that discipline. But I was stubborn. I was still uncertain with my math skills and so, decided to pursue Business Intelligence since not only do I have passion for Data, but because this field can branch out to Artificial Intelligence, Which are my main interests at the time of this writing.



Then came the time for our summer break, my one and only opportunity to catch up on personal projects due to tight schedules on the days I have classes. It was at this time when I delved deeper into data science and pursued Machine Learning.

Yes, It does have a lot of mathematical concepts involved, especially calculus and linear algebra. But am I still scared?

My answer to that question, is a big, fat No. I have already built the tenacity to accept challenges that may come my way, and is even excited to experience them since I believe that challenges are the ones that make us grow beyond our limits.

I belong to the group of white-box thinkers since I prefer knowing how a thing works from the inside out. Thanks to my innate curiosity, I learned different mathematical algorithms used in machine learning and how each algorithms is used and when are they used.

I've always been the kind that prefers not looking at things by just its surface level, but having the curiosity to dig deeper, uncovering the real reason how that thing works at the foundational level.

Was this journey intense?

It CERTAINLY was, it was thanks to my resilience and determination to keep learning that I was able to push through, despite the challenges I have faced. My skills weren't the result of a single overnight success. It was the result of the accumulation of my compounding knowledge learned throughout my life, and the relentless pursuit of knowledge.

Which, brings us right back to where we started. Is it talent, or is it hard work? I'll let you be the judge.



"The only way to overcome your fears is to face them head-on."
